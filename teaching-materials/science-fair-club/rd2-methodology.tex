\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=mygray,colframe=black,title=Purpose]
This workbook will help you define the \textbf{independent variable}, \textbf{dependent variable}, and \textbf{controlled variables} in your investigation. Your goal is to clearly explain what you will change, what you will keep the same, and what you will measure.
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,title=Success Criteria]
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
    \item I can identify the independent, dependent, and controlled variables.
    \item I can explain how the independent variable will be changed.
    \item I can explain what will be measured for the dependent variable.
    \item I can identify 2--3 controlled variables and explain how they will stay the same.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Step 6: Look at the examples}

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X}

\begin{minipage}[t]{\linewidth}
\textbf{Level 1--2 example}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=mygray,colframe=black]
The independent variable is temperature. The dependent variable is how fast the tablet reacts. The controlled variables are the beaker and the tablet.
\end{tcolorbox}

\textbf{Why this is limited:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
    \item It names the variables, but does not explain them clearly.
    \item It does not explain how the independent variable will be changed.
    \item It does not explain exactly what will be measured.
    \item It does not explain how the controlled variables will stay the same.
\end{itemize}
\end{minipage}

&
\begin{minipage}[t]{\linewidth}
\textbf{Level 3 example}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=mygray,colframe=black]
The independent variable is the temperature of the water. I will change it by preparing water at 10$^\circ$C, 20$^\circ$C, 30$^\circ$C, 40$^\circ$C, and 50$^\circ$C. The dependent variable is the reaction time of the effervescent tablet, which I will measure in seconds. The controlled variables are the volume of water, the type of tablet, and the container. I will keep these the same by using 100 mL of water, the same tablet each time, and the same beaker for every trial.
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{minipage}
