Teaching and Learning

Philosophy of Education

Students must feel safe to be ambitious.

I believe learning begins with safety. Students should feel free from judgment about their identity, ability, or background so they can take risks, participate fully, and pursue challenge with confidence.

Structure enables risk-taking.

Clear expectations, consistent routines, and explicit guidance reduce anxiety and help students get started. When students know what success looks like, they are more willing to engage in challenging work.

Learning is built through formative feedback.

I use ongoing assessment to surface misconceptions, guide instruction, and support student self-efficacy. Mistakes are treated as information, and progress is understood as non-linear.

Students should leave feeling capable.

My goal is for students to develop a sense of mastery and confidence in their ability to relearn, persist, and solve problems independently, even when material is difficult.

Equity requires intentional practice.

I actively work to create an inclusive classroom by listening, learning, and ensuring that all students see themselves reflected and respected in the learning environment.

Rigor and support must coexist.

High academic expectations are paired with scaffolding, clear modelling, and opportunities for revision so that all students can access and succeed in meaningful learning.
