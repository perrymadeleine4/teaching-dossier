Introduction

About this dossier

This website acts as a digital version of my professional teaching dossier. It is organized around the main components of my professional learning: my teaching beliefs, philosophy of education, professional learning plan, classroom artifacts, and evidence from my teaching practice.

The selected materials are intended to show not only what I have created, but how my thinking has developed as I have planned, taught, assessed, reflected, and revised my practice.

Teaching and Learning

Core beliefs

Students build confidence through challenge.

I believe students grow when they are supported through productive struggle, given clear expectations, and encouraged to see difficulty as part of learning.

Assessment should clarify learning.

I aim to use assessment as a way to make learning goals visible, identify next steps, and help students understand what quality work looks like.

Classrooms should feel safe and structured.

Predictable routines, respectful relationships, and thoughtful scaffolding help students feel secure enough to participate, take risks, and improve.

Reflection

Professional growth

Across my B.Ed. program, I have developed a stronger understanding of how planning, instruction, assessment, and reflection work together. I have learned to design lessons with clearer learning goals, create more transparent assessment tools, and respond more intentionally to student needs.

Moving forward, I want to continue strengthening my ability to support diverse learners, use formative assessment effectively, and create mathematics and science classrooms where students feel both challenged and capable.
